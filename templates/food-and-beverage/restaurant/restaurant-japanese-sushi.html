Our Story

Discover the tradition and passion behind Sushi Sakura.

A Tradition of Excellence

Sushi Sakura was founded by Master Chef Hiroshi Tanaka, who brought his culinary expertise from Tokyo to create an authentic Japanese dining experience. After years of training under renowned sushi masters in Japan, Chef Tanaka established Sushi Sakura with a commitment to honoring traditional techniques while embracing seasonal ingredients.

Our restaurant is designed to evoke the serene beauty of Japan, with minimalist décor, natural materials, and subtle touches that create a tranquil atmosphere. The name "Sakura" pays homage to Japan's beloved cherry blossoms, symbolizing beauty, renewal, and the fleeting nature of life—principles that guide our approach to cuisine.

At Sushi Sakura, we believe in the Japanese concept of "Omotenashi"—wholehearted hospitality that anticipates guests' needs. Our team is dedicated to providing an exceptional dining experience, where every detail is thoughtfully considered, from the presentation of each dish to the ambiance of our dining room.

Omakase Experience

Trust our master chefs to create a personalized dining journey with the finest seasonal ingredients.

The Art of "Omakase"

Omakase, meaning "I leave it up to you," is a traditional Japanese dining experience where guests entrust the chef to create a memorable meal. At Sushi Sakura, our Omakase experience showcases the finest seasonal ingredients and our chefs' creativity and expertise.

Each Omakase course is thoughtfully prepared and presented, taking you on a culinary journey through various flavors, textures, and cooking techniques. From delicate sashimi to perfectly seasoned sushi rice and creative hot dishes, every element is crafted with precision and care.

Our Omakase is served at the sushi counter, where you can interact with our chefs and watch as each dish is prepared before your eyes. This intimate setting allows for a personalized experience, as our chefs adjust each course based on your preferences and reactions.
